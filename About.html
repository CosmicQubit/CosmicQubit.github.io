My Artwork

Most of my artwork consists of digital drawings and renderings. I figured I should explain what equipment and software I like to use.

Digital drawings

For digital drawings, I use a fairly basic drawing table I bought off of amazon. Its the Huion h1611 drawing tablet. It cost me about 80GBP on Amazon which I imagine is quite a lot for most people. However, it's one of the cheaper options and definetly worth the price. If you are considering getting into digital drawing then do not bother with the super expensive ones with screens. The difficulty with drawing tablets is not learning how to use the tablet but learning how to draw. Digital drawing feels different from traditional drawing, for many reasons.

When it comes to software, I tend to use different ones. I use coral painter 2020 and 2021, paint but, mostly, I use Photoshop. Now, all of that is ridiculously expensive stuff I managed to get lucky with. However, there is also a free software I ocassionally like to use too called Krtia and it's absolutely wonderful to use. Not as powerful as photoshop, but you don't need it to be. It has all the necessary features.

Rendering

Rendering is a completely different process from digital drawing. It's like an even more technologically evolved version of it. That's probably a bad way to describe it actually. But for it, I use a free software called Blender. It allows you to do anything, the more professional (and super expensive) softwares let you do, from 3D modelling to animation. It's essentially how companies like Pixar and Disney make their 3D animated films. The technicallity of it is really complicated and can be daunting but there are so many tutorials on youtube that can help you learn. You can easily pick up the basics in a few days from them, which is what I did.

Of course, if you really want to be able to do anything with rendering, you need the technology for it. Which is why I had to invest in an expensive laptop for it. I use the Lenovo Legion i7 15IMHg05. It cost me about 1500GBP and while no one has to invest that much, you do need decent pc power otherwise you're gonna have to wait quite a while to render, deal with overheating and lots of lagging etc... That's essentially the biggest, and for most, the only major downside to rendering. But, all my awesomer looking artworks come from there, so for me, it's worth it.
