Biography

"I'm a 3D Artist"

Cosmic Qubit is a Burmese born artist based in Cardiff, Wales. They are currently studying engineering at Cardiff university. Their background in science and technology made 3D and digital software the perfect medium for their artwork. Their artwork tend to focus on topics and themes they find facinating such as Space, photorealism and nature. A lot of the visuals of their artwork are also heavily inspired by their love of science fiction and fantasy.

My Artwork

Most of my artwork consists of digital drawings and renderings.

I've been doing digital artwork since the age of 7 using  Adobe and Paint on my windows XP computer before I even moved to the UK. But it was only in 2020 that I got into 3D art as well. I am thoroughly facinated by technology and use art as a medium to explore what it can accomplish. There's no denying that I am a nerd, I grew up reading/watching/playing everything science fiction and fantasy. I studied physics, maths and computer science and chose engineering as a career alongside doing art. I have a deep love and facination with space and nature and I try to reflect that in all of my work.

Most of my work are inspired by the fiction and non-fiction I consume on a regular basis. I like to take inspiriation from my own interests and try to recreate the feelings in my own artwork. My artwork tend to focus on subjects and ideas that I am personally invested in such as astronomy, science, nature, technology etc. However, my style tends to vary from photorealistic to stylized, scientific to fantastical, bleak to colourful. Art for me is a way of exploring all these different ideas so I like to approach them from various angles, changing my style is one method I use to achieve this.

My Equipment

I do most of my work on my personal computer. Rendering my 3D artworks require a lot of computational power so I use a powerful setup that consists of an RTX2080 Super Max-Q, 7 10875H (8 cores, 16 threads) and 16gb RAM. I primarily use the Eevee and Cycles render engine on Blender for my artwork and do the post-render editing across various softwares depending on my needs. I also have a simple drawing tablet that I use to do any digital drawing I may need.
